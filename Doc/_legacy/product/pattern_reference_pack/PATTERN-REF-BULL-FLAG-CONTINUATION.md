# PATTERN-REF-BULL-FLAG-CONTINUATION

## Status
Reference document for the intended V1 continuation-pattern scope.
This file is not runtime proof.

## Pattern identity
- Canonical id: `BULL_FLAG_CONTINUATION`
- Domain label: `BullFlagContinuation`
- Family: continuation pattern
- Bias: bullish after confirmation
- Typical context: sharp advance followed by a short, orderly pullback or sideways drift

## Core reading
A bull flag is a continuation structure built from two parts:
- a strong upward impulse, often called the flagpole
- a slower counter-trend consolidation, often drifting slightly downward or sideways, called the flag

The usual interpretation is that buying pressure dominated the impulse and that the pullback reflects temporary consolidation rather than a true trend reversal.

## Structural conditions
A pattern candidate should not be treated as a bull flag unless all of the following are materially present:
- a meaningful impulsive rise exists first
- the post-impulse consolidation is shorter and less aggressive than the pole
- the consolidation slopes modestly downward or stays relatively flat
- the consolidation does not destroy the core bullish structure of the preceding impulse
- the pattern remains a pause after strength, not a full trend reversal sequence

## Situations to distinguish
### Clean momentum continuation case
A strong pole followed by an orderly shallow pullback supports the classic bull-flag reading.

### Weak impulse or deep pullback case
The pattern thesis becomes weaker.
A deep retracement can signal that the structure is no longer a simple continuation pause.

### Sideways market without prior impulse
Do not force a bull-flag interpretation.
The prior impulsive move is central to the pattern identity.

## Detection reading
Useful observable facts:
- visible impulse leg higher
- relatively contained counter-trend pullback
- consolidation channel or mini-range against the prior move
- momentum cools, but the structure does not fully reverse

## Validation reading
A bull flag becomes materially stronger when:
- price closes above flag resistance
- breakout direction matches the prior bullish impulse
- breakout is accompanied by stronger volume or other confirming evidence
- price holds outside the flag instead of immediately falling back into it

## Invalidation reading
A continuation reading weakens or fails when:
- price breaks below the lower support of the flag
- pullback becomes too deep or too disorderly relative to the pole
- breakout attempt fails and price re-enters the flag
- momentum evidence no longer supports a bullish continuation thesis

## Target logic
A common educational measuring rule is to project the flagpole height from the breakout level.
That is a planning framework, not a promise.

## Risk and advice guidance
### When the flag is still forming
Advice should emphasize patience and monitoring.
The bullish idea exists, but confirmation is still missing.

### When breakout above resistance occurs
Helpful guidance usually focuses on:
- confirming a close beyond flag resistance
- checking whether the breakout is supported by stronger volume
- monitoring whether the breakout holds instead of fading immediately

### When price breaks below flag support
Advice should shift from continuation to invalidation risk.
A bearish failure of the flag is incompatible with a confirmed bull-flag continuation reading.

## Common mistakes to avoid
- calling any pullback after a rally a bull flag
- ignoring whether the prior move was truly impulsive
- ignoring volume context
- entering before confirmation
- confusing a major reversal with a temporary flag consolidation

## V1 wording guardrails
- Do not call the pattern confirmed while price remains inside the flag.
- Do not ignore flag-support failure.
- Do not present the flagpole projection as certainty.
- Do not treat a deep structural breakdown as a still-valid bull flag.
