# PATTERN-REF-SYMMETRICAL-TRIANGLE-CONTINUATION

## Status
Reference document for the intended V1 continuation-pattern scope.
This file is not runtime proof.

## Pattern identity
- Canonical id: `SYMMETRICAL_TRIANGLE_CONTINUATION`
- Domain label: `SymmetricalTriangleContinuation`
- Family: continuation pattern
- Bias: bilateral structure, continuation only after confirmed breakout in the direction of the prior trend
- Typical context: price compression after an existing directional move

## Core reading
A symmetrical triangle forms when price creates lower highs and higher lows inside two converging trendlines.
The structure reflects compression and temporary balance between buyers and sellers.
In a continuation interpretation, the prior trend matters and the breakout must confirm direction.

## Structural conditions
A pattern candidate should not be treated as a symmetrical-triangle continuation unless all of the following are materially present:
- a meaningful directional move exists before the triangle
- local highs compress downward while local lows compress upward
- both converging bounds are touched multiple times
- the range visibly narrows as price approaches the apex area
- the structure reads as compression, not as a broad unstable chop zone

## Situations to distinguish
### Uptrend before the triangle
The continuation thesis is bullish only after breakout above the upper boundary.

### Downtrend before the triangle
The continuation thesis is bearish only after breakout below the lower boundary.

### No meaningful prior trend
The pattern may still exist structurally, but the strict continuation interpretation is weaker.
A bilateral reading is safer than a continuation claim.

## Detection reading
Useful observable facts:
- lower swing highs
- higher swing lows
- converging boundaries
- contracting price space
- direction unresolved until breakout

## Validation reading
A symmetrical-triangle continuation becomes materially stronger when:
- breakout is confirmed by close outside the boundary
- breakout direction matches the prior trend
- volume or other confirmation tools support the move
- price holds outside the triangle instead of immediately falling back inside

## Invalidation reading
A continuation reading weakens or fails when:
- breakout direction opposes the intended continuation case
- breakout occurs but price rapidly re-enters the triangle
- triangle quality is poor because the bounds are too loose or too inconsistent
- the structure persists without decisive resolution and loses informational value

## Target logic
A common educational measuring rule is to project the full triangle height from the breakout point.
That is a reference projection, not a certainty.

## Risk and advice guidance
### When the triangle is still compressing
Advice should remain neutral and patient.
The key message is that compression is visible, but direction is not yet confirmed.

### Bullish continuation case
Helpful guidance usually focuses on:
- confirming a close above the upper trendline
- using the prior uptrend as context, not as proof by itself
- watching for hold-above behavior after breakout

### Bearish continuation case
Helpful guidance usually focuses on:
- confirming a close below the lower trendline
- using the prior downtrend as context, not as proof by itself
- watching for hold-below behavior after breakout

### Weak or ambiguous breakout case
Advice should explicitly mention bilateral risk and false-breakout risk.

## Common mistakes to avoid
- assuming the prior trend guarantees breakout direction
- entering before confirmation
- ignoring broader market context
- ignoring volume and other confirming tools
- treating any converging shape as a clean symmetrical triangle

## V1 wording guardrails
- Do not state direction while price is still inside the triangle.
- Do not present the prior trend as sufficient confirmation.
- Do not suppress the bilateral nature of the structure.
- Do not present projected height as a guaranteed destination.
